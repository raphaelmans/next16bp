# Open Play (Social Booking)

## Purpose

Open Play is a social feature that turns a single reservation into a group activity. Instead of one player paying the full court cost, multiple players share a session and split the expense. It makes courts more affordable and helps players find others to play with.

## How It Works

### Creating an Open Play Session

1. A player or venue owner creates a regular court reservation.
2. After booking, they have the option to convert it into an Open Play session.
3. They set the number of available spots and a suggested cost split per player.
4. The session becomes publicly visible on the Open Play listing page.

Alternatively, a session can be created standalone without an existing reservation.

### Joining an Open Play Session

1. A player browses the Open Play listing page (accessible per venue or globally).
2. They see session cards showing: sport, date/time, available spots, cost per player, and organizer info.
3. They request to join a session.
4. The organizer approves or declines the request.
5. On approval, the player is added to the participant list.

### During the Session

- All participants can chat within the session (real-time messaging).
- The organizer can manage participants: approve pending requests, remove players, promote external participants.
- Participants can leave the session voluntarily.
- The organizer can close the session when it is full or complete.

### Player Reporting

For safety, participants can report other players for inappropriate behavior. This is a moderation feature.

## External Open Play

The platform also aggregates Open Play sessions from external booking platforms (e.g., Reclub).

**How it works:**
- External sessions are displayed alongside internal ones but with distinct "External" labeling.
- These sessions have lower trust indicators since the organizer is not verified on KudosCourts.
- Players can still view details and request to join, but the flow is different from internal sessions.

**Business purpose:** Increases the supply of play sessions even before the platform has enough organic activity. Players benefit from seeing more options.

## Open Play Listing

**What the player sees:**
- List of upcoming sessions at a specific venue (or globally)
- Filter by date range
- Session cards with sport, date/time, spots remaining, cost share, organizer
- Clear distinction between KudosCourts sessions and external sessions

## Business Value

- **For players:** Makes courts more affordable by splitting costs. Solves the "I want to play but I do not have enough people" problem.
- **For venues:** Increases court utilization — courts that might sit empty can fill through group sessions.
- **For the platform:** Creates network effects. Each Open Play session is a micro-community that brings players back.
