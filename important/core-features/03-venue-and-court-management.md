# Owner Listing & Venue Operations

## Purpose

This is the guide-aligned owner view of the product: get the venue found with a strong public listing, then layer in the operational setup that makes bookings possible.

## Current Guide Narrative

The latest owner-facing public guidance emphasizes three points:

1. Discovery comes before operations.
2. A listing needs enough detail for players to trust it.
3. Owners should keep control over pricing, operations, and booking policy.

This doc therefore starts with listing quality and only then moves into the back-office systems behind it.

## What A Strong Venue Listing Includes

The owner-facing venue surfaces collect the details that matter most to public discovery:

- venue name
- exact address and map placement
- supported sports
- photos
- contact details
- amenities
- organization branding when present

The listing is valuable even before every operational system is fully configured, because players still need enough detail to decide whether the venue is worth opening or contacting.

## Trust And Visibility Signals

Players trust a venue more when the listing looks complete and when the platform can show stronger status signals.

Current visibility and trust controls include:

- venue verification status
- public venue detail pages
- reservation-enabled status for verified venues
- QR code generation that points to the public venue page

Verification is especially important because it changes how confidently the venue can be presented to players and whether online booking can be turned on.

## Courts As The Real Inventory

Courts are the bookable units behind the public venue page.

Per-court configuration includes:

- court label
- sport
- optional tier/quality label
- active status

A venue can be discoverable before every court is fully booking-ready, but a court is only operationally useful for online reservations once it has the required availability and pricing setup.

## What Makes A Court Booking-Ready

A court is effectively ready for online booking when it has:

1. active status
2. schedule coverage
3. pricing rules

Supporting owner tools behind that readiness include:

- court-hours editing
- schedule normalization for overnight windows
- availability studio for exception management
- add-on configuration

## Operational Tools Behind The Listing

Once discovery quality is in place, owners can keep building toward go-live with:

- venue and court editing flows
- booking imports for migration and blocking existing commitments
- owner dashboard surfaces that expose setup, reservations, and operational warnings

These are not the first thing the public guides lead with, but they are the systems that turn a visible listing into a working booking surface.
