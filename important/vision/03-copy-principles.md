# Copy Principles

Guidelines for all user-facing text — landing pages, product UI, marketing, and communications.

## Audience Split

- **Primary (80%):** Players — Filipino sports enthusiasts looking for courts
- **Secondary (20%):** Venue owners — court operators wanting more visibility

Always lead with the player message. Venue messaging is secondary and compact.

## Discovery Language

### Use (Discovery Verbs)
- discover, find, search, explore, browse, check
- "discover courts," "find your next game," "explore venues"

### Avoid (Booking/Transaction Verbs)
- book, compare, shop, buy, purchase, subscribe
- Exception: "reserve" is acceptable when describing the specific action of requesting a time slot

## Key Framing Rules

| Concept | Do | Don't |
|---------|-----|-------|
| Platform identity | "discovery platform" | "booking platform," "marketplace" |
| Availability | "check availability when venues manage it" | "real-time availability" (implies all venues) |
| Venue value prop | "full control" | "no lock-in" (negative framing) |
| Pricing | "free for venues" | "no fees" (sounds defensive) |
| Reviews | "real player reviews" | "verified reviews" (implies verification process we may not have) |
| Reservation | "reserve your slot" (when applicable) | "book online" (implies guaranteed booking) |

## Tone

- **Confident** — state what we do, don't hedge
- **Direct** — short sentences, active voice, no jargon
- **Warm but not cute** — approachable without being silly
- **Filipino context** — reference real cities, real sports, real player habits

## Pain Framing

Always frame pain in terms the player recognizes:
- Scattered Facebook pages
- Separate reservation sites for each venue
- No way to read reviews in one place
- Messaging owners and waiting for replies

Never frame pain as a competitor attack. Frame it as the status quo that everyone knows is broken.
