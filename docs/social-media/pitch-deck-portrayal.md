# Pitch Deck Portrayal (Venue Owners)

This is the “owner deck” framing: simple, practical, and trust-focused.

## Core narrative

- Problem: bookings are stuck in DMs/spreadsheets → conflicts + wasted time
- Solution: publish availability + accept reservations in one dashboard
- Trust: claim + verify protects venues
- Clarity: free for venues; no payment processing
- Ask: claim/list your venue

## 6-slide outline (copy + talk track)

### Slide 1 — Title

Title: KudosCourts  
Subtitle: Court discovery for players. Free reservation system for venues.

Talk track (10s):
“We help players find courts and help venues manage reservations—without payment processing.”

### Slide 2 — The problem

- Bookings in DMs and spreadsheets don’t scale
- Double bookings and missed messages happen
- Players can’t see real availability

Talk track (20s):
“This isn’t a preference problem; it’s a tooling problem.”

### Slide 3 — What we do

- Publish availability as bookable slots
- Standard reservation statuses + owner confirmation
- Built-in chat for coordination

Talk track (20s):
“Less back-and-forth, fewer conflicts.”

### Slide 4 — How owners get started

- Create owner account
- Add or claim your venue
- Verify → enable reservations when ready

Talk track (20s):
“You control when bookings go live.”

### Slide 5 — Trust + payments

- Verification protects your venue from fake owners
- No payment processing
- Venues keep existing payment methods; players pay venues directly

Talk track (25s):
“Reservation system first. Payments stay with you.”

### Slide 6 — Call to action

- Claim/list your venue: kudoscourts.com/owners/get-started
- Optional: “Reply VERIFY for the link.”

Talk track (10s):
“If you manage a venue, start here—free for venues.”

## Q&A (fast replies)

- Is it free? → “Core listing + reservations are free for venues.”
- What’s the catch? → “We aim to sustain via ads/sponsored placements—not subscriptions.”
- Do you take payments? → “No payment processing.”
- Why verify? → “To prevent fake owner claims and build trust.”
