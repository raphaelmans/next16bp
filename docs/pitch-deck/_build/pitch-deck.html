KudosCourts

Player-first discovery + reservations for sports venues.

Draft pitch deck (Markdown only) Date: 2026-01-20

Vision

KudosCourts is a player-first, location-based sports venue discovery and reservation platform.

  • For players: find the right place, see real availability, and request a booking in minutes.
  • For owners: publish hours, pricing, and slots - then manage bookings with lightweight ops tools.
  • For the platform: stay neutral (no in-app payments, no in-app messaging) while still enabling trust and accountability.

Problem

Players

  • Discovery is fragmented: social pages, chat threads, screenshots, and outdated pins.
  • Availability is unclear: "Is this court actually free?" often requires messaging/calls.
  • Booking is inconsistent: every venue has a different process, rules, and pricing.

Owners

  • Scheduling is manual: spreadsheets + DMs + phone calls = errors and double-booking risk.
  • Pricing and policies are hard to communicate consistently.
  • "Going digital" typically means expensive custom software or awkward tools.

Solution

A single platform that connects discovery to bookable inventory.

  • Public discovery layer: places, courts, sport filters, location filters, amenities, and schedule views.
  • Standardized reservation engine: timeboxed requests, clear statuses, and an owner confirmation loop.
  • Curated-to-bookable pipeline: seed inventory via curation, then convert venues through claiming and verification.
  • Owner ops: hours, pricing rules, slot publishing, and a reservations inbox for fast triage.

Product Model (How The Platform Thinks)

Core entities (v1.2)

  • Place: a venue/listing (address, map pin, photos, amenities). Can contain multiple courts.
  • Court: a bookable unit inside a place. Constraint: 1 court = 1 sport.
  • TimeSlot: 60-minute inventory rows per court.
  • Reservation: a booking request that follows a mutual-confirmation contract.

Key mechanics

  • Duration is built from consecutive 60-minute slots (60/120/180+ minutes).
  • "Any available" can select the lowest total price across eligible courts.
  • Bookability is gated (curated, claimed, verified, enabled) to prevent low-trust bookings.

Player Journey

Discovery to booking

  1. Discover places by sport + location (and optionally amenities).
  2. Open a place detail page and choose:
    • A specific court, or
    • "Any available" (best price option).
  3. Choose duration (60/120/180+), then select a start time.
  4. Request booking (clear reservation status + next steps).

Paid bookings (P2P-style)

  • The platform does not process payments.
  • Players receive venue payment instructions in the reservation context.
  • Payment is timeboxed, then the player marks payment and the owner confirms.

Owner Journey

From setup to daily ops

  1. Create an organization.
  2. Add a place (venue) and one or more courts.
  3. Configure:
    • Day-specific hours (including overnight)
    • Hourly pricing rules
  4. Publish 60-minute slots (inventory).
  5. Manage reservations:
    • Inbox-first workflow
    • Court/place filters
    • Fast confirm/reject actions

Payment methods (org-scoped)

  • Owners can define one or more payment methods (wallet/bank), with per-method instructions.
  • A single default method is highlighted to reduce player confusion.

Trust, Safety, And Quality Control

A staged supply pipeline (reduces platform risk)

  • Curated places: discoverable, read-only, contact info visible, not bookable.
  • Claiming: owners can claim curated places through an admin-reviewed workflow.
  • Verification: owner-created places can require admin verification (documents) before bookings are enabled.

Privacy and enforcement

  • Payment account details are never exposed via public endpoints.
  • Payment methods are shown only in the reservation payment context.
  • Booking is server-enforced: unverified or disabled places cannot be booked.

Liability posture

  • No in-app payment processing.
  • No in-app messaging.
  • Clear expectations via reservation status + audit trail.

Go-To-Market (Draft)

Wedge

  • Start with one sport focus (pickleball), multi-sport ready by design.
  • Seed supply through curated venue listings to win discovery early.

Conversion loop

  • Players use discovery and drive demand.
  • Owners see demand and claim listings to unlock bookings.
  • Verified + enabled venues publish real inventory, improving the discovery experience.

Channels (examples)

  • Local club partnerships, tournaments, community groups.
  • SEO for "courts near me" and city/province discovery pages.
  • Venue outreach: "claim your place" and "get bookable" onboarding.

Business Model (Draft)

Near-term focus

  • Win on discovery and booking conversion (player-first).
  • Convert supply through curated listings -> claim -> verify -> enable bookings.

Monetization options (choose/sequence)

  • Sponsored placements / featured venues in discovery.
  • Owner subscription for advanced ops (multi-user staff, analytics, automation).
  • Booking fees / payments (only if the product later chooses to take on payment processing).
  • Advertising (if/when it fits the UX).

MVP

  • Monetization is intentionally flexible while we validate supply + repeat bookings.

Roadmap (Highlights From Current Plans)

Top-of-funnel improvements

  • Province/city discovery filters + search routing.
  • Amenities filtering.
  • Public schedule view + mobile sticky CTAs.
  • Resume-after-login so guests can return to their selection.

Trust + supply expansion

  • Place claiming improvements.
  • Place verification (owner request -> admin review -> enable bookings).
  • Guest removal requests (quality control).

Booking + ops depth

  • Organization payment methods + reminders.
  • Reservations inbox UX (owner triage).
  • Court setup wizard (hours/pricing/slots flow).
